Case Management

1. Reviews documented requests and assesses family circumstances to insure the individual/family applies for and/or receives all DHS services for which they are eligible.

2. Evaluates and resolves all discrepancies in case records and/or reports timely

3. Maintains an organized workflow.

4. Logs, updates, and maintains pending case actions as required

5. Documents/narrates all eligibility factors and pertiinent information timely

6. Responds to client inquiries and phone calls timely and accurately

7. Provides mentoring/technical assistance to other county office staff

8. Conducts second party reviews accurately and timely according to Division/Office policies and procedures

9. Assists with covering additional caseloads due to vacancies/absences and/or area projects

10. Establishes and maintains electronic case records (# scanned and % correct during rating period)

11. Processes over/under payments, inquiries, disqualifactions, correspondence, and communications with a 95% accuracy rate (includes transfer requests, fair hearing packets, client notices, emails from supervisors, ACD reports & judgments, etc.)

12. Processes all over/under payments, inquiries, disqualifications, correspondence, and communications with a 95% timeliness rate (includes transfer requests, fair hearing packets, client notices, emails from ADC reports and judgments, etc.
